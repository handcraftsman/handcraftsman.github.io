MovedApr 1855Anderson Valley, Mendocino Co., CA
``In April of 1855, a group of Swiss immigrants travelled from a settlement in Illinois, across the Donner Pass to Napa Valley, and then on to Cloverdale. They eventually arrived in the Anderson Valley in November of the same year. Among these pioneers was John Gschwend, his wife Elizabeth, and their daughter Christine. Christine, who was born in 1857, is said to be the first white child to be born in the Anderson Valley. By 1865, they had established a general store, a post office, a lumber mill, a grist mill, and a brewery. They named this community "Christine".''
``Charmed with its great beauty, and full of faith in regard to its climate, soil, and whatever else goes to make up happy homes, this pioneer pushed on into the very heart of the vale and there pitched his tent, about one mile west of the present site of Booneville. Here Mr. Anderson lived for a number of years, and had the pleasure and satisfaction of seeing the wisdom of his choice more than verified During the same year, 1852, Mr. J. D. Balls came into the valley and settled It does not appear that any more families came into the valley until 1855 [then] came John Gschwind [sic] [and] William Prafcher In 1856, James S.Smalley; during and before 1857, but exact dates not known, Oscar Carey, Joseph Gschwind,[sic] James Burgess, Henry Wade, Frank Buster, Cleveland Nellen, A. Guntley, A. Kendall, John Gosman, John Conrad, Alfred Braden, James Shields, W. W. Boone, A. Elliott and H. Stevens''
``In 1854 he went to Kansas, and in 1855 came to California across the plains with ox-teams, bringing also a band of cattle. He came direct to Mendocino county and began farming.''

``In 1856 he erected a grist-mill in Anderson valley, and as there were no roads then all the machinerey had to be packed to the mill site. He soon after added a sash saw, and finally put in a circular saw and a steam engine.''

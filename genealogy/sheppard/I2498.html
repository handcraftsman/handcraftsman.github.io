Obituary9 Feb 1940Windsor, Essex co., Ontario, Canada
"John A. Magee Dies in Windsor
Tilbury, Feb 9 - John A. Magee, a native of Tilbury East township and for may years a resident of this town, died at his home in Windsor on Wednesday evening following a long period of failing health and confinement to Grace Hospital, Windsor, since Dec. 24th of the past year. Early in January he underwent a serious operation from which he appeared to be improving a week ago but a turn later dissipated hope of final recovery. he was removed to his home 24 hours before the end came.
A son of the lat Mr. and Mrs. Henry Magee, John A. Magee lived in Tilbury where he pursued the trade of carpenter and contractor for a number of years prior to his removal to Windsor 20 years ago. He had served on the local municipal council and was a public-spirited citizen.
His immediate survivors are his wife, formerly Miss Maggie Davis of Tilbury East, a daughter Mrs. Edward Hornick, Guelph, a son Morley Magee, Windsor, two sisters and a brother, Mrs. A. Shaufler, Tilbury, Mrs. Newton Ainslie and James H. Magee, Blenheim and five grand children.
The remains which are resting at the Morris Funeral Home, Windsor, will be conveyed to this town on Saturday afternoon for the public funeral service in the Darrel S. Moffat Memorial church at 2:30 o'clock and burial in Maple Leaf cemetery this town."
Funeral12 Feb 1940Tilbury, Kent co., Ontario, Canada
"J. A. Magee Borne to Rest
Tilbury, Feb 12 - Impressive funeral rites were held here on Saturday afternoon for John Alexander Magee, native of Tilbury East township, who died at his Windsor home following an extended illness. The Rev. W. J. Marnes conducted an impressive service in the Darrel S. Moffat Memorial Church at which a large congregation of former neighbors and friends from town and vicinity joined the bereaved relatives who accompanied the cortege from Windsor, where a brief service had been held in the Morris Funeral Home the previous evening.
For his message of consolation to the bereaved ones the minister used the words, "Father, into Thy Hands I commend my Sprit." Mrs. Clarissa Scott Wylie sand "The Old Rugged Cross"
Immediate mourners were Mrs. Maggie Magee, the wife, Morley Magee, the son, and Mrs. Ed. Hornick, the daughter; Mrs. A. Shaufler and Mrs. Newton Ainslie sisters and James H. Magee, brother.
The brethren of Coronation Lodge I.O.O.F. conducted the graveside ritual in Maple Leaf cemetery, Pall-bearers were Archie and Stewart Shaufler, Gordon Ainslie, Norval and Otto Davis and Herbert Hornick."

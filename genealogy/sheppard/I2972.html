Birth1865IN
Death9 Mar 1922<Springfield, Clark co., OH>
Census7 Jun 1870Cambridge City, Wayne Co., IN
Census11 Jun 1880Springfield, Clark co., OH
Mentioned19 May 1887Cambridge City, Wayne Co., IN
``George Weast has engaged with E. f. Deiterichs, of Cleveland, Ohio, as traveling salesman in the territory of Eastern Indiana and Western Ohio, to sell Valve-Oleum, a machine oil. Hope George will succeed.''
Mentioned10 Sep 1891Cambridge City, Wayne Co., IN
``George Weast and family, of Springfield, Ohio, have been here visiting parents, this week. Mr. Weast has been at Richmond, for ten days, introducing and operating the new steam street roller, just purchased by that city.''
Mentioned5 Oct 1905Cambridge City, Wayne Co., IN
``Mr. and Mrs. George A. Weast, of Springfield, Ohio, enroute home from a visit to friends at Indianapolis and Connersville, spent Tuesday in this cisty, and met many friends of other days when Mr. Weast was a happy boy and bright young man. And strange to say he doesn't grow old. The cares of business are many, but he conducts them with that successful even temper conducive to long life and enjoyment.''
Mentioned1 Oct 1908Cambridge City, Wayne Co., IN
``Among those from a distance in attendance at the funeral of Mrs. Geo. L. Weast Sunday, was her son, Robert Elder, Sr., and Robert Elder, jr, and family of Indianapolis; Mr. and Mrs. Ben Stalker, who left the show to Michigan; Ad Sullivan and wife, of Urbana, Ohio; Chas. Davis and wife, of Marion; George L. Weast, Scott Weast and wife and George Weast, jr., and wife, of Springfield, Ohio, and Mr. And Mrs. Ed Fosdick, Indianapolis.''
Mentioned25 Mar 1909Cambridge City, Wayne Co., IN
``Relatives in attendance at the funeral of the late William Hamilton, on Sunday afternoon were George Weast and wife, of Springfield, Ohio; James Drake and daughter, Frank and Will Drake and wives, and Walter Ripley, of Indianapolis; William Beck, son and daughter, Robert Hamilton and wife, Mrs. Gertrude Myers, Mrs. Thornt. Taylor and Alex. Hamilton of Connersville.''
Mentioned7 Apr 1910Cambridge City, Wayne Co., IN
``George Weast, of Springfield, Ohio, was here with relatives over Sunday. He was also in search of machinists to take a job in the Ohio town and offered liberal wages and steady employment to several of the best machinists in the factories here.''
Census25 Apr 1910Springfield, Clark co., OH
Mentioned22 Jun 1916Cambridge City, Wayne Co., IN
``George L. Weast, a native of Cambridge City, and who, like all others, still retains his love for the lod town, came from his home in Springfield, O., Saturday afternoon, and after giving attention to a business matter, spent all his time untile late Sunday meeting friends and reviewing incidents of long ago. He reports his father, not withstanding his 88 years, as being in fairly good health, but needs two canes in walking. His brother, Scott Weast, and family are doing well.''
Mentioned11 Sep 1919Cambridge City, Wayne Co., IN
``George Weast and wife and Mr. Tremble and wife of Springfield, Ohio, were here Sunday with Mr. and Mrs. Horace Hamilton, enroute home from a week's visit with Mr. and Mrs. Edward Fosdick and Geo. Weist, sr., and taking in the fair at Indianapolis.''
Census13 Jan 1920Springfield, Clark co., OH
Mentioned30 Oct 1919Cambridge City, Wayne Co., IN
``George Weast and son, enroute Saturday from Springfield, Ohio, to Indianapolis to visit his aged father Geo. L. Weast, spent a few hours here with old friends, who enjoyed his visit even though it was brief. His father who will be 90 years old in November, is in rather feeble health. He is one of the few men living who served in both the Mexican and Civil wars. His home for several years has been with his daughter Mrs. Ed. Fosdick in Indianapolis.''

Property7 Aug 1679Farnham Parish, Old Rappahannock Co., VA
7 Aug 1679, John Meador and wife Elizabeth of Farnham Parish, Old Rappahannock co., Va sold 320 acres on Hoskins Creek to Ebenezer Stanfield, it being the "same granted unto Thomas Meador in 1663, father of said John."
Richard White, father of Elizabeth, witnessed this deed.
Court1684Old Rappahannock Co., VA
1684, John Meador of Rappahannock co., Va made claim for self and horse for 9 days for bringing corn and lumber from Rappahannock Indians Fort to the riversite for the county.
Property1689Old Rappahannock Co., VA
John purchased an additional 105 acres of land adjoining his 450 acre plantation on the south.
Property1689Old Rappahannock Co., VA
John received 190 acres adjoining his 450 acre tract for the transportation of 4 persons to the colony.
Property21 Apr 1690Old Rappahannock Co., VA
1690 Apr 21 - Old Rappahannock Co., Va - PAtents No. 8:40
John received a grant of 50 acres for the transport of one person to the colony. This land adjoined his land as well as that of Henry Awbrey on the main swamp of Hoskins Creek.
Will10 Dec 1694Essex Co., VA
John Meadors, Sr., widow, in the county of Essex, for the love I have for my children had by my wife Elizabeth, deceased, I give them all of the land had by my wife to be divided equally between them, as near as I can divide it. To my son Richard Meador and my son John Meador, Jr., a parcel of land beginning at Cole Springs, running to the great branch to the main swamp. to my son Thomas MEador land by John Evans. land on the east side of the great branch to my son Hope Meador. To daughter Rachel Meador, 105 acres bought of Edward Thacker also land given to me by my father-in-wal Richard White. land also to daughters Elizabeth and Esther Meador. All land given to sons and daughters never to be sold but to remain from heir to heir as long as there can be one of the Meadors found alive.

1662 Apr 13 - Old Rappahannock Co, Va - (1656-1664), pt.2
p.187-188: I, Sarah Meader, widdow wife of Thomas Meader the Younger
{Orphan] late decd in County of Rappahannock do bind myself to confirm unto
my son John Meader Sone unto Thomas Meader lately decd one yoke of oxen and
1 gun seaven (7) foot by the barrell to be delivered unto my Son John Meader
when he comes of age and
Furthermore I give as my full act and deed unto my eldest daughter Susanna
Meader Daughter unto Thomas Meader lately decd one red cow called Cherry and
her cow calf and increase to my aforesd daughter on excepting the male of
the catle to fall unto me the said Sarah Meader until my daughter be full
eleven (11) years of age and after the expiration of the aforesd years male
and female to fall unto my eldest daughter Susanna Meador.
Item: I give unto my youngest daughter Mary Meader Daughter unto Thomas
Meader lately decd one black heaffer named Slippey she and her increase to
my youngest daughter only the male excepted to fall unto I the sd Sarah
until my youngest daughter be fully 9 years of age and after the expiration
of nine years male and female to fall to Mary and if it shall please God
that my eldest daughter should dye in her minority that all her catle and
their increase shall fall unto her sister Mary Meader and likewise in case
it should please God that my daughter Susanna should dye in her minority
that all her catle and their increase shall fall unto her sister Mary and
Furthermore in case that my youngest daughter Mary her heiffer should
miscarry before she comes to perfecion that thee shall another heirffer or
cow of the same age and her increase to be made good as aforesaid to
daughter Mary - These Premises to be recorded in Court the 1st or 2nd Court
ensuing the date hereof by any person that I, Sarah Meador shall appoint
always provided that there be several marks and recorded the above childrens
catle and their increase this the 13th day of April 1662 /s/ Sarah (T)
Meader (her mark, a large T).
Wit: /s/ Abraham Coombe, /s/ William Clawson

Old Rappahannock Co, Va - (1656-1664), pt.2, p.188:
I, Sarah Meador late wife of Thomas Meador decd in County of Rapp:
oblige myself to give and allowe unto my children a sufficient and compleat
maintenance in every respect as all other ought to do and further more to
give them four (4) years a peice Education in learning according as the
county doth aford. Signed, sealed this 12 July Ao.Dom.1662 - /s/ Sarah (T)
Meader

1670 Apr 15 - Old Rappahannock Co, Va - (1670-1672), p.9 - Recorded: 12 May
1670
Know Ye that I, Henry Awbrey and Sarah my wife for valuable consideration
to us paid do graint and make over for us or heires all right tytle and
interest of 300 acres lying in County of Rapp. on North side of said River
of Rappahannock unto Francis Gowre for ever which land was formerly the land
of THOMAS MEDOR. . . to have and to hold 300 acres unto him Francis Goure
which the said Gowre is now SEATED on and possessed with all. . . forever -
Signed this 15 April 1670
/s/ Henry Aubrey, /s/ Sarah (T or F) Aubry
Wit: Thomas (x) Jenkins
/s/ John (I) Evans (his mark, large I with bar across middle)
Witnesseth that I, "SARAH AWBREY the NOW WIFE of Henry Awbrey" of Rapp. do
make and appoynt Mr. Thomas Gouldman my true attorney and in my name to
acknowledge in County Court of Rapp. all my right and title claim and
interest of 300 acres of land unto Francis Gowre
/s/ Sarah (F) Aubrey (large F or T)

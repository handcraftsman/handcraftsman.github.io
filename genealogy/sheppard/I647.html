Court26 Jun 1792Chester Co., SC
State vs Austin Culp - Bastardy
The defendant being bound to answer the charge appeared and confessed the fact and find the sum of L3-12
Court27 Jun 1792Chester Co., SC
State vs Austin Culp - Bastardy &C
The defendant who was fined for the said offence and he with Thomas neely his security made their signatures for the said fine and Court costs.
signed: Austin Culp, Thos. Neely
Court24 Jan 1793Chester Co., SC
A deed of conveyance from Austin Culp and Agnes his wife to Andrew Jeter was acknowledged in open court and ordered to be recorded.
Court29 Jan 1793Chester Co., SC
State vs Austin Culp - Bastardy &C
The defendant being formerly fined for the offense in the sum of L3-12 and Thomas Neely security for the payment of the said fine, whereupon the parties appeared in court instead of the said Thomas Neely who is Exonerated. Philip Walker Esquire came into court and engaged for the payment of the aforesaid fine in three weeks from this date and William Whitesides entered for the same and all the parties made their signatures for their performance thereof with costs (margin note: Exon Issued Clerks fee 9)
signed: Austin Culp, Philip Walker, William Whitesides
Court30 Jan 1795Chester Co., SC
State vs John Patterson - A battery committed on the body of Austin Culp
On a presentment of the Grand Jury. Whereupon the defendant comes into court and puts himself upon the mercy of the court and confesses the charge. Whereupon it is considered that he make his fine by paying the sum of 20/ and has indulgence unto the next Intermediate Court (Marginal note: Exon Issued 14th Sept 1795)
Court27 Jan 1797Chester Co., SC
Morgan Regan vs Austin Culp - Peton in Debt Nonsuited the Deft by his Counsel having pleaded Minority.
MovedAbt 1807Abbeville Co., SC
Austin Culp and wife left Chester co., SC about 1807 and moved to Abbeville co., SC.

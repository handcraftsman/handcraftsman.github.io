Obituary22 Mar 1914Kirksville, Adair Co., MO
``John B. Moncrief
John B. Moncrief was born in Jefferson county, Indiana, April 1st, 1832, died at his home in Gibbs Friday afternoon, March 20, 1914, aged about eighty-two years.
Uncle John, as he was called by all the younger people, was an old and respected citizen of Wilson Township, having resided at old Wilsontown where he was the village blacksmith for a number of years. He moved to Gibbs about eighteen years ago where he still followed his old trade until the past four years when old age compelled him to give up hard work and he sold his shop to his son, Edward.
He was united in marriage to Mary E Denton October 20, 1870 who, with four sons, Frank of Stuttgart, Arkansas, Thomas of Basworth, Missouri, Edward and Earl of Gibbs, and one daughter, Mrs. Minnie Brackney of Hurdland, Missouri; also one age sister, Mrs. Margaret Story of Gibbs are left to mourn his departure.
He was an old soldier and served his country faithfully in CO. A 37 Indiana Infantry.
Uncle John was a man who's word was always considered as good as any man's bond and he will be greatly missed by his many friends.
Funeral services will be held in the Christian Church of which he was a member, Sunday afternoon at 2:30, conducted by Rev. H.A. Browning of LaPlata and his body was laid to rest in the Union cemetery near Gibbs, Missouri.
Kirksville Daily Express Sunday, March 22, 1914''

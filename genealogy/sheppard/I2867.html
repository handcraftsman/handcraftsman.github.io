Events

Birth<1747>Currituck co., NC
Death<7 Feb 1799-1803/4>GA
Military11 Jun 1776Halifax co., NC
"Caleb Montcrief appears on the roll of the Halifax Brigade, North Carolina Line Thursday June 11 1776. He had the rank of ensign. His name, corretly spelled, appears in teh North carolina Treasurer and Comptrollers Records for 1782 and in other documetns in the North Carolina archives"
MovedAft 1776GA
"Maxa and Caleb went to Georgia after the Revolution"
Property12 Nov 1778Halifax co., NC
"Israel Judge sold Caleb Muncreef of Halifax county 100 acres for 500 pounds in the Granville Tract, southside of Hill Bridge Road. Nov 12, 1778 Halifax Conveyances"
Tax1782Halifax co., NC
"Caleb appears on the 1782 Tax list as worth 127 pounds and as the owner of one slave."
Spouse1779Halifax co., NC
m. Mary Daniel (abt 1761-bef Feb 1796), dau of William Daniel, and had: Arthur, Delilah, David (1783), Rebecca, Caleb Jr., John, and Wiley
Property28 Feb 1783Halifax co., NC
"John and Tabby Mitchel sold land to Caleb - 148 acres for 70 pounds Feb 28 1783. This acreage was located on the Licking Branch, Halifax Conveyances"
Tax1783Halifax co., NC
"On the 1783 list he again appears, worth 140 pounds."
Mentioned6 Feb 1796Halifax co., NC
"A Halifax county will by William Daniel, Feb 6 1796, indicates that Caleb (called Calip Crief) had married one of his daughters."

``
State of Indiana
Jefferson County
On the 13th day of Nov 1835 Personally appeared in open court before the Probate Court of Jefferson county Remembrahce Williams, a resident of Jefferson county state aforesaid aged 77 years who being first duly sworn according to law doth on his oath make the following declaration in order to obtain the benefit of the act of Congress of the 7th Jan 1832.
That he as a private served under the following XXX officers viz: General McIntosh, Colonel Crawford, Captain Archibald Wiggins, Lieutenant John ?Earson? and Ensign John P???ty.
Was drafted som time in Augist and in September started out from Hampshire county Virginia where he was drafted, where he lived at the time he was drafted, within five miles of the Town of Romney.
Marched thence towards Fort Pitt. Thence to the south of Big Beaver where they assisted in building Fort McIntosh. Thence to Tuscarawa where they build Fort Lawrence. Thence back to Fort McIntosh thence back to Hampshire county Virginia and received his discharge which he afterwards lost. This discharge was signed by McIntosh. This service was in the year 1778 or 79.
He cannot recollect precisely but is certain it was one of those years. The campaign lasted a period of six months.
Was engaged from almost the beginning of the War until the ?Pasture? of Cornwallis in assisting every way when called upon in guarding ?Mas??? Catching deserters and guarding them to block houses did catch one deserter which obsolved him of a tour of duty. Forgets when this was.
He was born about where the city of Washington stands in the year 1758. Was near 20 years of age when he performed his service. Knows of no living witnesses by whom he can prove his service.
He hereby relinquishes every claim to a pension or annuity except the ??? and dictates that his name is not on the pension roll of any state.
signed: Remembrance Williams''

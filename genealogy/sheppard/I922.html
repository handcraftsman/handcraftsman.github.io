Birth17 Jun 1739St. George's Parish, Spotsylvania Co., VA
Death5 Jun 1817Reddies River, Wilkes Co., NC
Mentioned10 Jan 1750St. George's Parish, Spotsylvania Co., VA
Robert was mentioned in his father's will dated 10 Jan 1750 in St. George's Parish, Spotsylvania co., Va
Property31 Aug 1751St. George's Parish, Spotsylvania Co., VA
31 Aug 1751, Benjamin Holloday of Spts co. and Susanna his wife, to Robert Shepherd of same county. Whereas sd. Benjamin Holloday had sold unto one George Shepherd [sr] a tract of 108 acres in Spts. co., and before conveying same the sd. George Shephrd [sr] died and by his will dated 10 Jan 1750 devised the sd. land to his sons, George Shepherd and Robert Shepherd part to these presents. Sd. Holloday and wife convey to sd. Robert Shepherd his share of the sd. land as under the will of the dead George Shephard [sr], etc. etc. Witnesses: Zachary Lewis, John Battaley, John Payton. recorded: 1 Oct 1751.
Spouse1 Oct 1765St. George's Parish, Spotsylvania Co., VA
m. Sarah Rash (23 Apr 1748 - 1829), dau of Joseph and Mary Rash, on 1 Oct 1765 in St. George's Parish, Spotsylvania co., Va, and had: Elizabeth, James, Nancy Ann, Mary "Polly", Agnes Jane, Rhoda, John, Sarah, Frances "Fanny", and Rebecca
Property20 Nov 1776Spotsylvania Co., VA
20 Nov 1776, Robert x Shepherd and Sarah his wife, George Sheperd and Mary his wife, of Spts. co., to Benjamin Holloday of same co., L54 currency 108 acres in Spts. co. Witnesses: Charles Yates, Edward Herndon, John Chew, Jr., Anthony G Holston, Clayton Coleman, John Herndon, John Holloday, Jr. recorded: 6 Jan 1779.
Moved7 Dec 1776Wilkes Co., NC
moved from Spotsylvania co., Va to Reddies River, Wilkes co., NC on 7 Dec 1776
Property24 Apr 1778Deep Ford, Reddies River, Wilkes Co., NC
Robert Shepherd entered 200 acres of land (Entry #64-24) beginning near Deep Ford on Reddies River [Wilkes co., NC] on John Shepherds line. John had entered 405 acres at the same time. At the time of entries, April 24, 1778, they had already made improvements on the property.
Tax1782Wilkes Co., NC
The 1782 tax list for Wilkes Co in Capt. Rowland Judd's District includes Robt. Shepperd with 200 acres of land, no negroes, 3 mules and horses, and 8 cattle.
Military1782Wilkes Co., NC
Robert Shepherd furnished 1 horse to the militia for which he was paid $1600 (voucher number 726)
Military1782Wilkes Co., NC
furnished 13 and 1 half bushels of corn to the Militia for which he was paid $60.00 in continental currency.
CourtApr 1784Wilkes Co., NC
John, James, and Robert Sheppard were appointed jurors for the April, 1784 court of Wilkes co., NC
Tax1784Wilkes Co., NC
Robert Shepperd listed with one poll and 300 acres of land in the 1784 Wilkes co., NC tax list.

Obituary8 Jan 1943Chatham, Kent co., Ontario, Canada
"James H. Magee Was Well Known In Tilbury Area
Tilbury, Jan 8 - The sudden death of James Henry Magee, a former resident of Tilbury East township and later of this town, came as a distinct shock to his many relatives and friends of this vicinity. Mr. Magee, who died at his Charing Cross home, had been under medical care for several weeks because of a heart condition and high blood pressure.
Born at Peterborough in 1868, the son of the late Henry Magee and Agnes Warnock, natives of the North of Ireland, he was brought to Tilbury East township in infancy. The family resided on their farm o the Foster Sideroad. At one time the later Mr. Magee was engaged in the grocery business in Tilbury and later carried on a butchering business here and also in Merlin. For 20 years he was engaged in real estate and farming in Alberta. Upon the death of his younger son, nine years ago, Mr. And Mrs. Magee returned to Tilbury and had resided in the county since that time.
Mr. Magee was a faithful member of the Anglican Church. Mr. Magee was twice married, his first wife being Miss Ida Funston, of Tilbury. He is survived by his wife, the former Miss. Lottie Glover of Chatham, a son Harold Magee, an Francisco, Calif.; two daughters, Mrs. P. Peterson, Calgary, Alta. and Mrs. Faurt, Rossland, B.C; a sister, Mrs. Albert Shaufler, Tilbury; six grandchildren and five great grandchildren.
The remains are resting at the Needham Memorial Chapel, Chatham, pending word from members of the family regarding funeral arrangements, when the rites will be conducted from St. Andrew's Anglican Church, Tilbury, by the Ven. Archdeacon T. Dobson. Interment will be in St. George's cemetery, Tilbury West townshipï¿½
